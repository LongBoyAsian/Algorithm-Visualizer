What is Huffman Encoding?

Definition: A popular greedy algorithm for lossless data compression. It assigns variable-length binary codes to characters based on their frequencies in the data. The most frequent characters get the shortest codes, while less frequent characters get longer codes.

Purpose: To significantly reduce the overall size of data (minimize the total number of bits) without losing any original information when decoding it back.

When to use: It is highly effective when certain characters appear much more frequently than others. It is commonly used as a back-end step in real-world compression formats like ZIP, GZIP, and JPEG images.
